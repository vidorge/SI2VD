History

A 212 began its life on November 12 in 1956., The premiere concert performance of Goethe's "Faust," directed by Mira Trailović, in which they played Mary Crnobori, Mata Milosevic, Viktor Starčić and Ljubisa Jovanovic, in the small hall of the old building, "Battles" in which there were only 212 chairs in the auditorium.
The emergence of Atelier 212 meant a turning point in the life of the theater not only in Belgrade, but also in this part of Europe. Atelier was founded as a theater in which to play the new avant-garde drama, of great influence in Europe at that time. In addition, they wanted to create theater that will be different from the existing organization, which primarily will be an institution. In search of suitable space in which to realize their ideas have found newspaper reading room of the house, "Borba", Borislav Mihajlovic Mihiz called the new theater: Atelier 212 crowd on the 12th of November in 1956. Applauded, and the public was receptive to this unusual theater with an unusually staged performances.
During the 54 years that followed, Atelier 212, through their performances, writers and directors, particularly through its great actor, has grown into one of the most important theaters in this region.
Atelier 212 is the first theater in Eastern Europe to have played "Waiting for Godot" by Samuel Beckett, in 1956. Was. The great success of this show has enabled a number of other avant-garde plays to appear in the theater. Sartre, Faulkner, Ionesco, Camus, Pinter, Adam, RUŽEVIĆ, Joyce, Jarry, TS Eliot, Vitrak, Schisgal, hooves, women, for the first time are displayed on the stage before the Yugoslav audience.
